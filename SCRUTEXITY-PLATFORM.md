## 1. Category: Claim Intelligence

**What Scrutexity actually does:**
Asks what claims a business makes, what evidence supports them, what needs to be fixed, and what changed over time.

**Category name:** Claim Intelligence

**Why this beats "Reputation Intelligence":**
- Reputation is broad — reviews, PR, brand sentiment, social mentions.
- Claim Intelligence is specific — every assertion a business makes, backed or not, tracked over time.
- Specific categories are defensible. Broad categories are crowded.

**Hero copy for scrutexity.ai:**

> Every business makes claims. Scrutexity shows which ones are real.
>
> Audit your site. Find unsupported claims. Create evidence-backed content. Monitor claim drift over time.
>
> Run a Claim Audit →

## 2. Platform Architecture

**The core object:** The Claim Record.

Not audits. Not content. Not monitoring. Every product module touches the same Claim Record.

| Claim State | Definition |
|-------------|-----------|
| Verified | Evidence found on the page or in benchmarks |
| Weakly Supported | Some evidence, insufficient for the claim's strength |
| Unsupported | No evidence found |
| Expired | Evidence was valid, TTL lapsed |
| Changed | Claim text modified since last audit |
| Revoked | Previously verified, now contradicted by new evidence |

This is the product. The living claim graph of a business.

### The three modules

| Module | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **AuditGPT by Scrutexity** | Find unsupported claims and proof gaps. Honest audits. No cheerleading. A real plan. |
| **Claim Rewrites by Scrutexity** | Turn verified claims into evidence-backed content. Not hallucinated marketing filler. |
| **Scrutexity Monitor** | Track claim drift, unsupported updates, expired proof, and reputation risk. |

## 3. The Medspa Question: Separate Vertical

The medspa revenue Retention wedge and the claim intelligence platform are two different companies:

- Different buyers (medspa owners vs. AI founders/agencies)
- Different sales motion (industry-specific pitch vs. horizontal SaaS)
- Different proof assets (Boulevard/Mangomint integrations vs. public audit reports)
- Different language (revenue Retention vs. claim verification)

**Recommendation:** Keep them separate. Scrutexity becomes the claim intelligence parent. The medspa business either:

- **Option A:** Stays as a separate vertical product under Scrutexity later, after the claim platform proves demand.
- **Option B:** Operates independently with its own brand and GTM.

Do not merge them. A platform that simultaneously sells "missed demand Retention for premium med spas" and "claim intelligence for AI businesses" will confuse the market and dilute both.

## 8. What Makes This Defensible

**The Claim Record is the moat.** Every audit, content brief, and monitor alert traces back to a persistent claim record with a content-addressed hash. Competitors can copy prompts. They can't copy the claim graph.

**The badge network compounds.** Every embedded "Verified by Scrutexity" badge links back to a public audit page. Every badge is an acquisition surface. Over time, the network of verified businesses becomes a distribution moat.

**The category is specific.** "Claim Intelligence" is not "reputation monitoring" or "AI content" or "website auditing." It's the intersection of all three. Specific categories are defensible. Broad categories are not.
