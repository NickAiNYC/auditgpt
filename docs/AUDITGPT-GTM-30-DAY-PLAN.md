### Recommended first wedge: **Seed–Series B AI/SaaS founders**

**Why this wins for fastest paid learning:**
1. They are *already* under buyer/investor trust scrutiny — claim risk is fresh and salient.
2. They are *findable in minutes* — public funding announcements, Product Hunt launches, YC batch pages, AI directories.
3. They are *founder-buyers* — no procurement, no committee. A $99–$299 spend is "yes" from one person.
4. Their sites are *claim-dense* by category — every audit produces a useful report. Repeatability is automatic.
5. They speak the language of the wedge fluently — "what claims can I prove" is already on their mind.
6. **Low legal/compliance risk** for AuditGPT — no clinical or medical exposure.
7. They are most likely to share their report publicly (because their site is the artifact they want to improve). Viral surface area is highest here.

### Backup wedge: **B2B SaaS agencies serving AI startups**

If the founder wedge produces snapshots but no $99/$299 closes within 14 days, pivot here. Same vertical knowledge, higher willingness to pay ($799 agency tier monetizes faster), but slower to find and slower to close. Use the founder snapshots as the demo material in agency outreach.

### What NOT to do first
- Do not lead with med spas. The compliance overhead is non-trivial and one mistake is a regulatory story.
- Do not lead with VC platforms. Long cycles, low-volume.
- Do not lead with D2C. Too many tooling alternatives; price compression.

## 3. The First 30 Audit Sprint

### Batch 1 — 10 AI/SaaS founders

**Goal:** Test whether founders pay for a claim/proof/AI visibility snapshot.

For each prospect:
- **Pre-outreach manual review (10 min/prospect):** Open homepage. Identify 1 claim that is overstated, weakly supported, or needs evidence. Identify 1 visibility gap (no clear "what does X do" answer, missing FAQ, no entity clarity). Identify 1 demand-leakage gap (high-friction CTA, no demo path, contact form only).
- **Snapshot angle:** Run the actual free snapshot through `/snapshot` after you DM them and they say yes.
- **Message:** Founder DM (see §4.1).
- **CTA:** "Want me to send you the 3-point snapshot?"
- **Reply qualifies if:** Any of "sure," "send it," "what's the catch," "how does this work."
- **Offer after reply:** Send the auto-generated snapshot. Then: "If the gaps look real, I'd recommend the $99 Starter Audit — 5–7 findings + a 7-day fix list. Want me to run it?"
- **Track:** Prospect → DM sent date → reply Y/N → snapshot delivered Y/N → $99 reply Y/N → close Y/N → which finding triggered conversion.

### Batch 2 — 10 agencies

**Goal:** Test whether agencies want white-label snapshot/audit deliverables.

For each prospect:
- **Pre-outreach manual review:** Open agency homepage. Identify 1 claim weakness *on their own site* (e.g., ranking guarantees, "we get you in ChatGPT," 10x revenue language). This is the demo material.
- **Snapshot angle:** Audit *their* site as proof of concept. Then offer to white-label for their clients.
- **Message:** Agency DM (see §4.2).
- **CTA:** "Want me to send you the snapshot on your site? You can see what your clients would see."
- **Reply qualifies if:** "Sure," "interesting," "what would white-label look like?"
- **Offer after reply:** Snapshot of *their* site → offer of sample audit on one of *their clients* → introduce $799/mo agency tier.
- **Track:** Same as Batch 1 + agency interest in $799/mo.

### Batch 3 — 5 med spa / wellness agencies

**Goal:** Test claim-risk messaging.

- **Pre-outreach review:** Open agency homepage AND one of their named client sites. Identify 1 claim-risk pattern (risk-free, permanent, best-in-city, FDA-approved).
- **Snapshot angle:** "Med spa marketing has unique claim-risk patterns. I run a free 3-point snapshot. Want me to send one on a client site as a sample?"
- **CTA:** "Want a sample snapshot on a client?"
- **Reply qualifies if:** They share a client URL or accept the offer.
- **Offer after reply:** Snapshot on client → introduce $99/$299 → introduce $799 agency tier.
- **Track:** Reply Y/N, claim-risk pattern that landed.

### Batch 4 — 5 fractional CMOs / consultants

**Goal:** Test whether audits work as a discovery artifact.

- **Pre-outreach review:** Open their LinkedIn + 1 client site if visible. Identify 1 claim or visibility gap on the client site.
- **Snapshot angle:** "Could AuditGPT be a useful discovery artifact for your next client engagement?"
- **CTA:** "Want me to run a snapshot on one of your client sites — would it work as a discovery deliverable?"
- **Reply qualifies if:** They say yes or ask follow-up questions.
- **Offer after reply:** Snapshot → discuss $99 Starter as their client deliverable → discuss white-label option.
- **Track:** Reply Y/N, whether they'd resell at $99/$299.

## 5. Free Snapshot Delivery Template

The snapshot the LLM produces follows the schema in `src/lib/audit-schema.ts` and renders through `/audit/[publicId]`. Output structure:

```
1. Claim / Trust Gap
   - claim found (verbatim from the site)
   - status (weakly supported / needs evidence / overstated / unsupported)
   - what we noticed (one observational sentence)
   - why it matters (one sentence)
   - quick fix (one specific action)
   - safer rewrite (one sentence, optional)

2. AI / Search Readability Gap
   - buyer question (what an AI / search query would ask)
   - observed gap
   - why it matters
   - recommended fix
   - suggested FAQ (one Q, optional)

3. Demand / Conversion Gap
   - surface (cta / contact form / phone / booking / follow_up / review_request / no_show / dormant_leads)
   - observed gap
   - recommended fix

Priority Recommendation
   - first fix
   - rationale

Recommended Scrutexity Next Step
   - product (Contento / AI Visibility / Recovery / Proof & Reputation / Agency White Label)
   - rationale
   - CTA

Disclaimer (mandatory)
```

### Founder-facing version
Open with: *"Three things I noticed on [domain] today. None of them are legal or compliance calls — just observations about how the page maps the gap between claim, evidence, AI/search readability, and demand."*

Lead the snapshot with the claim gap. Founders index on claims.

### Agency-facing version
Open with: *"Three things I noticed on [domain]. Same three I'd flag for any of your clients in this category. If you want to white-label this format, ping me."*

Lead the snapshot with whichever gap is sharpest. End with the agency white-label note.

### Med spa / wellness-facing version
Open with: *"Three things I noticed on [domain]. AuditGPT does not make legal, clinical, or compliance calls — it observes where claims, evidence, AI/search readability, and demand paths could be tightened."*

Lead with the claim gap (where the legal risk lives). Always include the safer framing rewrite. Always end with the disclaimer.

## 8. Contento Internal Dogfood Loop

Every AuditGPT report should produce a Contento project. Even before Contento is a paid product, this proves the loop and generates marketing assets.

### Workflow

1. **Run AuditGPT** on a target site.
2. **Select top findings.** Pull the 3 strongest claim findings and the 1 strongest demand-leakage finding.
3. **Create a Contento project folder** (Notion or Google Drive) named `Contento — [company name] — [date]`.
4. **Generate a governed brief** from the findings: approved claims, unsupported claims, forbidden claims, safer rewrites, FAQ opportunities, proof blocks.
5. **Generate assets** (the part that becomes a paid Contento product later):
   - Safer hero rewrite
   - One FAQ block
   - One proof page outline
6. **Use the assets in AuditGPT marketing.** Each Contento project becomes a case study showing what the next step actually delivers.
7. **Track which content converts** — log which assets generate the most snapshot upsells.

### First 30-day targets (realistic, not aspirational)

|---|---|---|

| Prospects contacted (DMs sent) | 30 | 50 |

### Quality metrics to log

- Most common finding type per vertical
- Strongest message angle (by reply rate)
- Top objection
- Recommended-next-step clicks per audit
- Which specific finding triggered each paid conversion

### Decision rules

**Double down on Batch 1 (founders) if:**
- ≥3 of 10 founders reply to the first DM
- ≥1 founder converts to $99 within 14 days
- Replies index on "interesting" / "send it" / "what's the catch" (not "no thanks")

**Pivot to Batch 2 (agencies) as primary if:**
- ≤1 of 10 founders reply
- An agency reply leads to a $799 conversation within 21 days
- Founders aren't paying, but they are sharing snapshots publicly (still a viral signal)

**Pause and tune product if:**
- Snapshots are delivered but recipients say findings feel generic
- 2+ recipients say "this is just an SEO audit"
- The recommended next step routing feels wrong 2+ times
- Replies include "where's the proof you can actually do this"
