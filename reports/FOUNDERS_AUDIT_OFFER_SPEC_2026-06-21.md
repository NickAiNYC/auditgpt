## 1. What it is

A ruthless, no-cheerleader outside audit of a business and its owner, delivered as a structured deliverable with visual artifacts and a real growth plan. Not a claim checker. Not an AI-fluff report. The honest thing a sharp operator would tell you if they looked at your whole business and owed you the truth.

**What makes it different from the AI-cheerleader tools:** the AuditGPT output contract enforces it structurally — banned filler words, two-sentence cap per field, "insufficient data" instead of invented numbers, every sentence carries a fact. The honesty is engineered, not promised.

---

## 2. The deliverable (report structure)

A 10–14 page report. Each section is brutal about the *business*, never the *person*.

1. **Verdict** — one-paragraph honest read. Where this business actually stands. No hedging.
2. **The Owner Context** — who runs this, what their background (public LinkedIn) tells us about strengths and blind spots, used *to tailor the plan*, never to corner them.
3. **Claim & Evidence Audit** *(AuditGPT core)* — public claims scored: verified / weak / unsupported / overstated. Gaps reported as visibility gaps, never legal verdicts.
4. **Positioning Teardown** — what they say they are vs. what the market hears. The sharper version of their positioning.
5. **Market & Competitive Read** — who they're really competing with, where the white space is, what the category is doing.
6. **The Leak Map** — where demand/trust/revenue is leaking (visibility, proof, follow-up, conversion).
7. **Visual Artifacts** — see §3. The things they'll actually screenshot and use.
8. **The Growth Plan** — structured, prioritized, real. 30/60/90 with owners and sequence. Not "post more on social."
9. **What I'd do first if this were mine** — the single highest-leverage move.

| Artifacts | Generate scorecard/map/roadmap visuals | Approve, sharpen |

The automatable 70% is why it scales to ~$499. The un-automatable 30% (judgment + plan) is why anyone pays.

## 7. What to avoid saying (safe doctrine)

Fined · sued · FTC · liability · non-compliant · illegal · false advertising · "clinically safe" · "compliance verified" · guarantees of ranking/revenue/bookings · contempt for the person. Brutal about the work, never the human. Research the owner to *help*, never to *corner*.
