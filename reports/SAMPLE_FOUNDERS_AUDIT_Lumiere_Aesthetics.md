---

## 1. Verdict

Lumière looks like a premium clinic and sells like a discount one. The brand photography, the space, and the injector's credentials signal high-trust, high-ticket care — and then the site buries the price, hides the practitioner, and asks a nervous first-time patient to "book now" with no reason to trust the needle. You are not losing to other med-spas on skill. You are losing to your own website's silence. The demand is arriving; it is leaking between "interested" and "booked."

---

## 2. The Owner Context

Public profile reads as a clinically strong, credentialed injector who came up through nursing/aesthetics — real expertise, real hands. That is the asset the entire business should be built around, and it is the asset the website mentions in one sentence on an About page nobody reaches. The blind spot is common for clinical founders: the site is built to describe *services* when the patient is buying *the practitioner*. The plan below leans into the founder's credibility because it is the strongest and most under-used thing here.

**Read:** The site makes premium *claims* with budget *proof*. For a treatment that involves a needle in the face, the proof gap is the conversion gap. Cautious patients don't disbelieve you — they just can't confirm you, so they bounce to the clinic that showed the work.

---

## 4. Positioning Teardown

**What the site says:** "A luxury med spa offering Botox, fillers, and skincare."
**What the market hears:** "Another med-spa. Same services as the 40 others within 3 miles."

You're competing on *service list* in a category where every service list is identical. The sharper position is the one thing the list can't copy: **the injector.** "Results that look like you on a good day, done by [founder] herself — not a rotating staff" is a position a chain can't claim and a patient actually chooses on. Right now you sound like a menu. You should sound like a person.

---

## 5. Market & Competitive Read

- **Real competitors:** not "luxury med-spas" — it's the 3–5 clinics in the same zip that *do show* before/afters, *do show* the injector's face, and *do* answer "how much" before the consult.
- **Where they beat you:** proof visibility and price transparency, not skill.
- **White space:** practitioner-led trust + radical pre-consult transparency. Most clinics hide behind "book a consult to discuss pricing." The one that says "here's roughly what this costs and here's who'll do it" wins the anxious first-timer — the highest-LTV patient.

---

## 6. The Leak Map

Demand is arriving (search, referral, social). It leaks here, in order of cost:
1. **Proof leak** — no before/afters → can't confirm quality → bounce.
2. **Trust leak** — practitioner invisible → "who's holding the needle?" → bounce.
3. **Price leak** — "book to discuss" → friction for a discretionary purchase → bounce.
4. **Follow-up leak** — no visible response on missed inquiries → the interested-but-not-now patient is never recovered.

Each is fixable in days, not quarters. None require more ad spend.

## 8. The Growth Plan (30/60/90)

**First 30 days — stop the proof leak (highest ROI, lowest effort):**
- Add 8–12 consented before/after sets to the Services pages. This is the single highest-leverage move.
- Put the founder's face, name, and credentials on the homepage, not buried in About.
- Publish a starting-price range per treatment. Transparency converts the anxious buyer.

**31–60 days — rebuild the position around the practitioner:**
- Rewrite home + services from "menu" to "practitioner-led results." Kill "premier," "thousands," "latest."
- Add 6–8 real reviews on-page with a visible count.
- Add a missed-inquiry follow-up so interested-not-now patients get a same-day response.

**61–90 days — compound it:**
- One short founder video: who she is, how she thinks about natural results. The trust asset a chain can't make.
- Structured review-request flow post-treatment to keep proof compounding.
- Track booking-rate before/after; expect the conversion gain to come from proof + transparency, not new traffic.

---

## 9. What I'd do first if this were mine

Put the before/afters and the founder's face on the homepage this week. Everything else is optimization; this is the leak that's costing you booked patients today. You already earned the demand. Stop letting your own website talk them out of it.
